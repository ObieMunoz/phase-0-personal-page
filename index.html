BIOGRAPHY

I was born and raised in Tampa, Florida, and went to a military school in St. Petersburg, Florida, called Admiral Farragut Academy. Although neither of my parents were in the military, as a child I was fascinated by those who chose such a career path. I thoroughly enjoyed my time at this school and feel it created a great foundation for who I am today. After I graduated from high school, I decided to stay local to the Tampa Bay, Florida, area and attended college at the University of South Florida.

During college, I bounced around several different areas of study until I ended with a Bachelor's degree in Criminology. I did not grow up wanting to learn about criminal behavior and I had no dreams of becoming a police officer. If I'm being honest, I was pretty lazy during college and I found that this was the easiest degree I could actually obtain. That being said, during my senior year of college, I completed an internship with a local sheriff's office. Who knew? Something I had never considered possible before happened: I chose to make a career out of it.

Since that time, I have spent my adult life over the past 10 years serving my local community as a law enforcement officer. After being hired, I received successive promotions and eventually became a Financial Crimes Detective. Over time, I investigated and managed an average of 26 cases simultaneously, primarily focusing on all forms of fraud including credit card and wire-transfer, the criminal use of personal identification information, financial exploitation of the elderly, business e-mail compromise scams, and miscellaneous other financial offenses.

Although I enjoyed the majority of my law enforcement career, in 2021 and after a decade of service, I decided I was ready to focus on a new journey. I resigned from law enforcement in May, 2021, and began learning to code in my free time. I started out by learning Python using free resources such as Python Principles and YouTube. While teaching myself as much as I could, I referenced many documentation websites and eventually completed a small amount of coursework on Udemy.

Eventually, I realized I wanted more. I wanted to learn to code at a faster pace than I could teach myself for free online so I began researching coding bootcamps and eventually found Flatiron School! I was impressed by what I heard after speaking with admissions and decided to take a leap, joining the cohort beginning on November 1, 2021!

After completing my pre-work requirements early, Flatiron staff reached out to me and asked if I would like to join the cohort that begins on October 11, 2021. There's no time like the present, so I accepted. I know my new career is waiting and I can't wait to have it within reach!
